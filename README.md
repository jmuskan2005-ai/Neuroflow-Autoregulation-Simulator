# Neuroflow-Autoregulation-Simulator
The NeuroFlow Autoregulation Simulator is a simple, math-based project that shows how the brain keeps its blood flow steady even when blood pressure rises or falls. It uses a smooth logistic model to mimic how blood vessels naturally widen or tighten and compares the behaviour of a healthy brain with that of a stroke-affected one.
This project is perfect for understanding the basics of cerebral autoregulation through a mix of biology, calculus, and visual simulation.

Your brain protects itself by keeping blood flow constant, no matter how your blood pressure fluctuates. Healthy brains do this very well. After a stroke, this ability becomes weaker.
This simulator visualizes this difference in a clear, easy-to-understand plot.
